The NFL Draft came to Pittsburgh April 23–25, 2026. Hundreds of thousands of people moved through the city over three days — by bus, light rail, car, bike, and on foot. City services were strained. Parking was chaotic. The rivers were there regardless.

This dashboard is a city-wide near-real-time civic data view built for the duration of the event. It pulls together the data feeds Pittsburgh already publishes — transit, traffic, parking, weather, river levels — and puts them on one map. It's not a fan app or an NFL product. It's a public-interest tool for locals trying to get around, press covering the event, and data-curious civic folks who want to see what's actually happening across the city, not just at the stadium.

Every feed is labeled with how stale it is. The goal was honesty: show what the data says, show when it was last updated, don't oversell it.

This project is the foundation for a more permanent tool — a city-wide event dashboard that can be reconfigured for any major Pittsburgh event: the marathon, Picklesburgh, a stadium concert, a winter storm.
